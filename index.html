◆ Inherited cardiac conditions
  • What are inherited cardiac conditions?

    What are Inherited Cardiac Conditions?

    Inherited cardiac conditions, or ICCs, are a group of heart problems that affect the electrical signals, and sometimes muscle function, of the heart. These conditions can be passed from parent to child and may not be recognized until a seemingly healthy individual has a serious heart event that causes loss of consciousness and in some cases death.

    Signs and symptoms

    1. Fainting spells or dizziness with exercise or emotion
    2. Heart racing or palpitations
    3. Seizures
    4. Fatigue and shortness of breath

    Family history of

    1. Sudden cardiac arrest
    2. Sudden cardiac death
    3. Heart failure
  • Common inherited cardiac conditions

    Common Inherited Cardiac Conditions

    Long QT Syndrome (LQTS)

    Long QT syndrome affects how long the heart takes to recover electrically between beats. This can lead to dangerous fast heart rhythms, fainting, and in some cases sudden cardiac arrest—often triggered by exercise, emotion, or certain medications.

    More information

    Brugada Syndrome

    Brugada syndrome is an inherited condition that disrupts the heart’s electrical signals. It is often identified on an ECG and may increase the risk of serious arrhythmias, particularly during sleep, fever, or in some people without symptoms until a cardiac event occurs.

    More information

    ARVC (Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy)

    ARVC is a condition in which heart muscle—especially in the right ventricle—is replaced by fat and scar tissue. This can cause palpitations, fainting, and life-threatening arrhythmias, and may progress to heart failure over time.

    More information

    CPVT (Catecholaminergic Polymorphic Ventricular Tachycardia)

    CPVT causes abnormal heart rhythms that are often brought on by exercise or strong emotions. The heart may look structurally normal on standard tests, but affected individuals are at risk of fainting and sudden cardiac arrest during physical activity or stress.

    More information

    HCM (Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy)

    Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy involves thickening of the heart muscle, most commonly the wall between the heart’s chambers. It can cause shortness of breath, chest pain, palpitations, and an increased risk of arrhythmias and sudden cardiac death, even in young, active people.

    More information

    May 28, 2026

    Update regarding your FLNC variant

    From: HiRO Genetics Team

    We are writing to let you know about a reclassification of a variant in the FLNC (filamin C) gene identified in your genetic testing. This variant has been updated from a variant of uncertain significance (VUS) to likely pathogenic, based on new evidence from recent research and family studies.

    FLNC-related conditions can affect the heart muscle and electrical system, and may increase the risk of arrhythmias or cardiomyopathy in some individuals. This change does not necessarily mean you will develop symptoms, but it may affect screening and follow-up recommendations for you and your family members.

    We recommend scheduling a follow-up appointment with your care team or a genetic counsellor to review these results in the context of your personal and family history. Please contact us if you have questions or would like to discuss cascade testing for relatives.
